Introduction

The risks of subsidence appear in areas where soluble rocks are present, namely carbonate deposits (limestone) and evaporite deposits (gypsum and anhydrite), where gaps and caverns appear within this type of geological deposits. They may collapse when the ground loads above them increase, either naturally or by human action, and these rocks collapse to form sinkholes and cause danger to the installations and infrastructure built on them. As for ground cracks and faults, they occur as a result of natural factors or caused by man. Among the natural factors that help in the occurrence of ground cracks and fissures are earthquakes and volcanoes, and they depend mainly on the nature of the geological structures and the quality of the rocks or soil. One of the human factors that contribute to cracking and cracking in the ground is the depletion of natural resources from the ground, such as water and mineral resources, which leave subterranean gaps and porous gaps in the ground, causing it to crack, split, and subside.


Objectives:

  • Awareness and protection from the negative effects resulting from the risks of landslides and cracks
  • Identifying dangerous places that are at risk of this type of geological hazard
  • Creating a database for the locations of the risks of landslides and cracks in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia
  • Educating the community about rationalizing groundwater consumption, using modern irrigation guidance systems, to avoid the negative effects resulting from the formation of ground cracks.
